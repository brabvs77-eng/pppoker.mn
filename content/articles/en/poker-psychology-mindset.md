# Poker Psychology — Avoiding Tilt

**Poker psychology** is half the game. You can know [hand rankings](https://pppoker.mn/en/articles/poker-hand-rankings/) and still lose your roll on tilt. Staying calm at [Baatryn Öröö](https://pppoker.mn/?lang=en) tables is a long-term edge.

## What is tilt?

Emotional decisions after bad beats:

- Playing too aggressive after a loss
- «Revenge» calls with weak hands
- Overconfidence after a win — playing too many hands

## Tilt warning signs

- Playing hands outside your chart
- Ignoring position
- Oversized pots
- Staying at a table when you should leave

Time to end the session.

## Stay calm — rules

1. **Session limit** — time or hand count (e.g. 2 hours)
2. **Stop-loss** — max daily loss (e.g. 5% of roll)
3. **Break** — 15 minutes after a bad beat
4. **Notes** — log what triggered mistakes

## Bankroll link

[Bankroll management](https://pppoker.mn/en/articles/bankroll-management/) supports mental game. Separate poker money reduces emotional swings.

## Playing from home in Mongolia

Phones and messages distract. During a session, focus on poker only.
