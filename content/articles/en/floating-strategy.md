# Floating — Advanced Online Poker

**Floating** means calling with position to take the pot later — often without a strong hand. Advanced play: learn [hand rankings](https://pppoker.mn/en/articles/poker-hand-rankings/), [position](https://pppoker.mn/en/articles/table-position-poker/), and [bluffing](https://pppoker.mn/en/articles/art-of-bluffing/) first.

## What is a float?

Opponent raises pre-flop; you call to see the flop. They c-bet; you call again, planning to win on turn/river or bluff later.

## When it works

- Dry boards (A-high, K-high rainbow)
- Opponent c-bets too much
- You have position (button vs EP)
- Heads-up or two-way pot

## Risks

- Too many floats bleed chips
- Plan to fold if they barrel turn
- Respect [bankroll limits](https://pppoker.mn/en/articles/bankroll-management/)

## Baatryn Öröö tables

Hold'em 9-max — float most from button/cutoff. Try at 250/500 when ready.

## Beginners

Master tight [starting hands](https://pppoker.mn/en/articles/starting-hand-selection/) first. Float after 500+ hands experience.
